About Us: Camping Overload

Camping is an outdoor activity involving overnight stays away from home in a shelter, such as tent or a recreational vehicle. Typically, participants leave developed areas to spend time outdoors in more natural ones in pursuit of activities providing them enjoyment. The night spent outdoors distinguishes camping from day-tripping, picnicking, and other recreational activities

Motor Homes or campervans is a self-propelled vehicle that provides both transport and accommodation. The term mainly describes vans that have been fitted out, often with coach-built body for use as accommodation. They usually have sleeping spaces for 2-8 people. Each sleeping space is either fixed or converts from another part of motorhome interior, usually a fold-out sofa. Kitchen area contains cooking equipment and the types of equipment used are different from motorhome makes and model but generally a kitchen has a stovetop, oven, refrigerator, and sink.

The history of recreational camping is often traced back to Thomas Hiram Holding, a British travelling tailor, but it was first popularised in the UK on the river Thames. By the 1880s large numbers of visitors took part in the pastime, which was connected to the late Victorian craze for pleasure boating. The early camping equipment was very heavy, so it was convenient to transport it by boat or to use a craft that converted into a boat.
